A Classic Whodunit: Murder on the Orient Express

Welcome to my review of Agatha Christie's masterpiece. If you love locked-room mysteries, you must read this book. When I first started reading, I thought I had the killer figured out by chapter three, but I was ~~so incredibly smart~~ completely and utterly baffled by the end.

The Setup

"The impossible could not have happened, therefore the impossible must be possible in spite of appearances." — Hercule Poirot

The story follows the brilliant and eccentric Belgian detective, Hercule Poirot, as he boards the luxurious Orient Express. A snowdrift traps the train in the mountains, and a wealthy American passenger is found stabbed in his locked cabin. You can explore more of Poirot's legendary history on the Official Agatha Christie Website.

Why It Works So Well

Here is why this novel has stood the test of time:

  1. The Atmosphere: The claustrophobic tension of a train stuck in the snow is perfectly executed.
  2. The Detective: Watching Poirot use his "little grey cells" to interrogate passengers is an absolute joy.
  3. The Twists: Every single clue, from a dropped pipe cleaner to a broken watch, matters to the final solution.

Minor Gripes

While it is incredible, a few things might slow down modern readers:

  • Keeping track of the dozen different suspects in the first act can be tricky.
  • Some of the untranslated French phrases require a quick search to understand.
  • The pacing in the middle consists almost entirely of back-to-back interviews.

Final Verdict

A spectacular, genre-defining mystery that earns a 9.5/10.
